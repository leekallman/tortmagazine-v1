story

Do we need tort magazine? Obviously no, I mean, what do we really need? But let’s skip that discussion. In many ways we actually need a magazine like tort. Not only due to my ego or reasons projected by the same. However, tort portrays engagement, movements and reformations in the society. Our platform aims to point out voices, which, directly or indirectly, can be heard in the discussions about societal issues. And let’s be clear about one thing – we all need to level up. Not to be interpreted that it’s sophisticated to join the trendy Swedish lifestyle (that is; you just do whatever a “healthy” influencer does, without any reflections). No. Instead, we are inviting our readers to broaden their perspective. Our readers must not necessarily be triggered to actively engage more in societal matters (however, please do). It’s quite more simple, like, let people finish their sentences. Talk to people who you haven’t yet talked to, and listen. That is basically the spirit wherein TORT was born. My name is Lizette Olofsson and I am the editor-in-chief of tort. I’ve always (!) wanted to work for a magazine, but due to a lack of courage, I went to law school. Today I work with legal tech, I know everything about Elon Musk and I spend hours reading documents from the European Commission. But that’s not everything. I’m also someone who is questioning stuff (my life) and get shit done (tort). Together with some of the most inspiring people I know, TORT was born – 100 % out of passion. That feeling will be manifested in our first issue, coming in May 2020.


Best, Lizette

A tort, in common law jurisdiction, is a civil wrong that causes a claimant to suffer loss or harm, resulting in legal liability for the person who commits a tortious . It can include the intentional infliction of emotional distress, negligence, financial losses, injuries, invasion of privacy and many other things.
